Equipment

The boys will need to wear shin guards and socks for practices and games. From ASA guidelines: "Shin guards must be worn under the socks, with the socks completely covering the shin guards." I highly suggest soccer socks, but I do not recommend the umbro socks at Walmart that my son might sometimes be wearing.

As far as shoes, ASA has said: "Soccer cleats are optional." The cleats need to be soccer cleats and not football or baseball cleats.

"Proper soccer shoes must be worn. No toe cleats are allowed. No metal cleats are allowed."

Ensure that the boys' shoes are double or triple knotted.

Soccer balls - Each boy should have their own size 3 ball for practice and before games. Showing up to practice with a size 1 or size 5 will not be the most helpful in preparing your child for games.

The referees will be checking these things and more at the beginning of games.

Games

Games will have referees and at half time, we will be swapping sides. Scores are not recorded with ASA, but I will usually keep score to see how competitive we are. I try to make sure all the kids get adequate game time.

I will not be able to be physically on the field. Therefore, I will need like 3 feet from the sideline to be free, so that I can run the sideline. I will have the portable sports bench during the games that I would like the boys to be sitting at in the case that I need to make a quick substitution. If they are not there, I might make the assumption that they aren't ready to go back in. If there is an issue with shoes, etc., I will not put them in the game or if they are in the game, I will substitute them out from the game before resolving the issue and possibly quickly getting them back in the game.

If someone wants to do a sign-up sheet (i.e. signup genius web page) for after game snacks, you are welcome to take the initiative and post it to the chat.
