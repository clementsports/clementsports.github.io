Practices

Development of the kids' skills is so important. I want each kid to be improving and understanding where I want them to be on the field. The kids need to be prepared for success and so that they have a less likelihood of getting hurt.

Equipment

The boys will need a protective cup, glove, helmet, baseball cap (will get one as part of uniform), bat, water bottle, and cleats (soccer, baseball, football, even turf will be okay, although I strongly recommend baseball/football ones).

The ASA rules state:
"Metal cleats are NOT allowed."
"All players must wear a protective cup for the groin."

Bats - "Youth League standard of 2-1/4" to 2-3/4" diameter but no greater than drop 12 (-12) and no longer than 32 inches in length." The umpire ensures that it will "conform to USSSA and or USA Baseball standards."
"NO SOFTBALL BATS WILL BE ALLOWED IN ANY LEAGUE OR DIVISION"

Games

Warm up will start 45 minutes before a game. If your boy wants to do extra warm up or has special things he want to do before the game, it is best to do it before the team warmup before the game, so the team can warm up together.

Scores will be kept. The rules are different for each age group and updated periodically.

I need to know if your son is not going to make it to a game. Please let me know sooner rather than later by updating RSVP on GameChanger. I have to plan for the game ahead of time and I also am busy preparing the boys for the game. If you are going to miss part of the game for any reason, please let me know too as that might mean (to the umpire) in certain circumstances that we would forfeit or receive penalty.

Every kid might be playing catcher at some point. Currently, the catcher equipment is provided by ASA.

Every kid most likely will be playing the outfield at some point.

The boys need to prove to me in practice that they can play a position to play it. The kids will probably not be playing the same exact field position throughout a game. As this is a recreational league, it's for their own development and I don't think it's appropriate.

There are a lot of rules in baseball and most are at the discretion of the umpire. If you see something off with the game, opponent, etc. and it's time sensitive, talk to me immediately during the game.

I will need parents to help with scorekeeping and helping the kids with the catcher's gear.

Scorekeeping

I have a scorekeeping book to record how both teams perform. One of the most important things to do is to ensure the other team follows their batting order. If we are the away team, ensure that the other team scores correctly on the scoreboard. If there is ever an issue or discrepancy, please come talk to me IMMEDIATELY!!

I understand that doing the scorekeeping is quite demanding and am grateful for the people helping out the team. I especially want to know the final score, score for each inning, and the number of bases each batter gains. The scorekeeping book is what determines the official score; the scoreboard does not. The scorekeeping book allows me to look back at the game and almost see what happened play by play. It would be great to see the inning score, the outs numbered in an inning, differentiate strikeouts from other outs, number of bases each hitter gains (e.g. 1 base, 1 base and overthrow, 2 bases, in-field home run, etc.). These things help me evaluate how the kids are doing.

During home games, we need to do the scoreboard. I prefer it to be the same person as the scorekeeping book so that they are in sync, but I will leave that up to the person doing the scorekeeping book.

The scoreboard app is DAK Score by Daktronics. If another person connects to it, the scorekeeper can not connect to it. An ASA 8U regulation game is 80 minutes.

Weather and Reschedules

The city of Allen makes the determination whether we use their baseball fields on the Rainout line (https://rainoutline.com/search/dnis/4692144255). On Saturday and Sunday, they make the determination around 7am the day of. On weekdays, they make the determination around 3pm. This makes the determination later than sometimes we want but also close to game time.

ASA might reschedule our field to a turf field at the last minute. For example, this might mean I think the game is cancelled due to the city of Allen at 3pm and then it is not cancelled due to the reschedule at 3:30pm. This is rare, but it has happened and want you to be aware of it.

You can ask me in the chat before the city makes their determination, but I will say I have to wait to hear what the official determination by the City of Allen or ASA is. I understand that it is unnerving to be held in limbo, but this is their process.

I make the determination on days where we practice at public parks. I usually follow what the city of Allen dictates. I have been known to change an outside practice to an inside practice at an indoor facilty. Usually this type of reschedule is done the day before after reviewing the weather forecast. Therefore, the time of practice will probabaly change too due to availability of the indoor facility. If we have an ASA scheduled Allen baseball field practice, I usually don't reschedule them to an indoor facility due to the scheduling complexity.

When it's cold and/or rainy, usually the umpire will allow the kids to have jackets on over their uniforms. I'm not sure if there will be a point when that will no longer be the case.
