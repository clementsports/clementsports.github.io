Equipment

The boys will need a protective cup, glove, helmet, baseball cap (will get one as part of uniform), bat, water bottle, and cleats (soccer, baseball, football, even turf will be okay, although I strongly recommend baseball/football ones).

The ASA rules state:
"Metal cleats are NOT allowed."
"All players must wear a protective cup for the groin."

Bats - "Youth League standard of 2-1/4" to 2-3/4" diameter but no greater than drop 12 (-12) and no longer than 32 inches in length"
"NO SOFTBALL BATS WILL BE ALLOWED IN ANY LEAGUE OR DIVISION"

Equipment Tips

Bat

It will depend upon the age and strength of your kid, ... As a possible starter bat, I would recommend a 24" bat with a -12 drop. One of the bat's we have is https://www.walmart.com/ip/Rawlings-RX4-USA-Youth-Tball-Bat-12-24-inch/660468610. Although it may not seem much of a difference with a larger bat, it has affected the performance of my son in the past. Hitting will be very important this season as there will be outs.

Another bat that I have is this or very similar: https://www.walmart.com/ip/Easton-MAXUM-Youth-Tball-Bat-25-inch-11/755195717. Here are some bat guides to get a better perspective: (https://www.rawlings.com/sizing-bats.html, https://www.dickssportinggoods.com/rc/baseball-bat-buying-guide).

Glove

For reference, my son uses a 10" baseball glove. I know other kids use smaller gloves (9" or 9.5"). Most important is that they feel comfortable catching with it.

Misc

I've bought stuff at Walmart, Play It Again Sports, Dick's Sports, Academy Sports, etc. ASA (and PSA) sometimes has coupons at Dick's. You don't have to break the bank to get good enough quality equipment for these kids.

Games

Please be early to the games for warm up. I normally arrive a half an hour before a game as I have routine of things I like the boys to do before a game. Scores will be kept. The rules are different for each age group and updated periodically.

I need to know if your son is not going to make it to a game by updating RSVP on GameChanger. I have to plan for the game ahead of time and I also am busy preparing the boys for the game.

Every kid will be playing catcher. The boys need to prove to me in practice that they can play a position to play it.

There are a lot of rules in baseball and most are at the discretion of the umpire. If you see something off with the game, opponent, etc., talk to me immediately during the game if it's time sensitive.
